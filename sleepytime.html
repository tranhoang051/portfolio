Wanderly Mobile App

Wanderly Travel is a comprehensive mobile utility curated to minimize logistical friction for modern independent travelers. By unifying algorithmic, context-aware discovery paths with absolute financial accounting mechanisms, users can transition instantly from high-level travel dreaming to organized execution.

Problem

Travelers often get overwhelmed juggling multiple planning tools. Scattered reservations, hard-to-read map pins, and confusing manual expense spreadsheets create frustration, leading many to give up before their trip even begins.

Objective

Wanderly targets these paint points by introducing clear itinerary timelines, smart automated budget summaries, and tailored destination finders. The goal was to build a cohesive interface that makes trip planning simple and transparent for both solo explorers and groups.

Problems (Key Pain Points)

01

Cost Tracking Chaos

Users find it difficult to track their actual spending distributions while moving from place to place.

02

Choice Overwhelm

Too much time is spent digging through mismatched advice and filtered review feeds to find authentic spots.

03

Rigid Itineraries

Static maps make it hard for travelers to tweak and update their routes when unexpected delays happen.

Research

User interviews and contextual research showed that travelers value immediate access to their daily expenses and route edits far more than social feeds. Observing real-world planning habits helped guide our emphasis on accessible layout structures.

Competitive Analysis

App Evaluated Identified Strength Identified Limitation
Tripo App Simple list timelines Lacks cost dashboards and unique location recommendations
Google Trips Excellent map pin sync Can feel cluttered; missing integrated budget controls

Target Personas

PROFILE A

Anna (24) — Freelance Content Designer

Goals: To pin scenic visual locations seamlessly while keeping track of daily food and accommodation expenses.

Pain Points: Annoyed by messy cost lists; hates switching between map apps and notepad documents.

PROFILE B

David (36) — Remote Project Manager

Goals: Wants to coordinate clear daily agendas and back-up schedules for his family in one central hub.

Pain Points: Easily stressed by unexpected transit changes; gets overwhelmed when itineraries are too rigid.

Wireframes

Early low-fidelity layouts focused on creating clear connections between dashboard routes, trip timelines, and expense charts before adding visual branding elements.

Test & Iterate

Usability testing highlighted that a plain list of expenses felt disconnected from the itinerary. To fix this, I added a dynamic donut chart to the dashboard, making it easier for users to see their budget breakdowns at a glance.

Reflection

Designing Wanderly highlighted the importance of user-centered iteration. It showed that complex planning tools can feel intuitive and stress-free when information hierarchy and visual balance are prioritized.
